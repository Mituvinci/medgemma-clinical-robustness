A 1-year-and-8-month-old healthy Hispanic male is seen at the clinic due to a 1-year history of multiple stable, hypopigmented and asymptomatic macules, and papules on his suprapubic area and axillae. He had no prior treatment and no relevant past medical history. On physical examination, the lesions were found to be well-demarcated, 2e4 mm, hypopigmented macules on the suprapubic skin, lower abdomen, and axillae. The histopathological examination of one of the lesion demonstrated the presence of several CK7-positive large clear cells (i.e. Toker cells) in the epidermis. Act as a dermatologist and determine what is the diagnosis.
